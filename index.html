How Kobrain Works

Three steps to having a teammate who works as hard as you do.

1

Create Your Kobrain

Give it a name, an email address, and define its role. What does it do? What's off-limits? You set the rules. Kobrain learns from how you work.

2

Send an email to your Kobrain. CC them on tasks. Loop them into threads. They'll read it, understand what you need, and get to work — using your actual tools.

Your Kobrain works through tasks, sends updates, and completes what you assigned. You review, redirect, and focus on the work only a human can do.

Questions?

What makes Kobrain different from ChatGPT or Claude?

Those are chatbots. Kobrain is a teammate — with its own email, its own memory, and access to your real tools. You delegate work via email. It does the work and emails you back. It's persistent, remembers everything, and operates in your actual workflow — not a chat window.

Can it really code and use GitHub?

Yes. Kobrain connects to GitHub, Vercel, Supabase, and other developer tools. It can write code, review pull requests, push commits, and manage deployments. Like a developer teammate who has access to your entire stack.

What if it makes a mistake?

Kobrain sends you updates before taking big actions. You set the boundaries — what it can do autonomously and what needs your approval. Like any teammate, you manage by outcomes, not micromanagement.

Is my data secure?

Kobrain operates within the integrations you authorize. It only has access to what you grant it. You can revoke access at any time. Your data is encrypted and we never use it to train models.
